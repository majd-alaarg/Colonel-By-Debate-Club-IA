About the Club

Colonel By Debate Club is ‘Arguably the Best’ club at Colonel By. Debate club helps you develop skills, from rhetorical strategies, to public speaking and confidence. Our weekly club hosts British Parliamentary debate rounds that cover relevant and specialized topics. Additionally, we host seminars and workshops to help you learn new skills, and develop your existing ones.

We also participate in tournaments hosted by universities such as UOttawa and McGill as well as attending regional, provincial and national tournaments where we compete against others from across the province and the country! Our club is open to first-timers, as well as experienced debaters. Your first debate can often be intimidating, but our club fosters a welcoming and friendly environment. Swing by for a fun afternoon of debating and learning.

Frequently asked questions

If you have a quick question, check to see if there's already a quick answer!

How do I join the Colonel By Debate Club?

There are many ways for you to join the Colonel By Debate Club. The first and most effective method is to sign up to our mailing list to receive weekly updates and emails regarding the club. Typically attached to messages are links to our Google Classroom and Google Meet codes for our weekly debate meetings, seminars, and information sessions. By signing up for our mailing list, you are effectively joining the Colonel By Debate Club and will have access to all the club events. The mailing list subscription form can be found at the bottom of this and every other page.

Do I have to be a Colonel By student to join CB Debate?

Of course not! Recently, due to the pandemic, we have opened up the club to high school students from outside Colonel By Secondary School. Anyone from the Ottawa region, preferably in the Ottawa Carleton District School Board, can attend our weekly meetings, tournaments and internal competitions. We now offer joint debate practice with other OCDSB schools! Simply sign up to our mailing list, found at the bottom of each page on our website, and you will be in the loop for future events.

How do I find tournaments to compete in?

Check out the “Competitions” page for more information regarding annual tournaments attended by CB Debate.

Also, OSDU (Ontario Student Debating Union) has many tournaments listed on their website along with their times and locations. They show upcoming tournaments at: https://osdu.org/events/

Some tournaments that are worth going to include:

  • McGill University Fall BP (late October)
  • Carleton University Xerxes Cup (early November)
  • Queen's University BP (early January)
  • McGill's Winter Bonanza (early February)
How do I sign up for tournaments as an independent team?

Look at the our “Competitions” page or the OSDU website for tournaments and look at social media posts from the organizations responsible for running the tournament you are interested in.

Once you sign up. There will be additional requirements such as payment. Usually, you will need to bring a supervisor (or parent or similar adult). Both you and that supervisor may have to sign a waiver.

Be aware that, as an independent team. you are not allowed to sign up under the school's name. A useful way of getting around this is to name your team "Kernel Bye", which allows other debaters to know which school you or without technically taking the school's name.

I’m going to my first away tournament, what do I bring?

We suggest that all debaters going on an away tournament bring the following items:

  • Normal clothes to change into
  • Debate clothes (standard, formal wear is preferred)
  • Paper, notepad, notebook, or other objects to write in
  • Multiple pens or pencils (in case if one gets lost or runs out of ink)
  • Personal hygiene items (toothbrush, face cleanser, etc.)
  • Personal devices (a phone is always useful not just to contact people but also to time yourself during your speech)
  • Something to do during downtime. In debate, there's a lot of waiting between rounds, so bring something that you can do during this time, such as homework, a book, an issue of the Economist (which also serves to intimidate other debaters in the room)
